## Q4 Product Summary

Q4 was a strong quarter for growth with one notable miss on revenue.

**The headline:** Monthly active users grew 21% to 5,100, and new-user activation hit 51% — up from 38% in Q3. The driver was the October onboarding redesign. Users who went through the new flow were twice as likely to still be using the product 30 days later. That's the most impactful single change of the year.

**The three features we shipped:**

The onboarding redesign delivered. The activation numbers show it clearly, and it also contributed to a modest churn improvement (down from 4.2% to 3.8% monthly). The bulk export feature had low adoption — only 11% of eligible users tried it — but the users who did were highly positive. It's a power-user feature, not a growth driver. Notification settings quietly did their job: fewer users opted out of notifications, which translates to more re-engagement over time.

**The miss:** Revenue came in at $48K MRR against a $55K goal. The shortfall was on Pro tier upsells, which moved slower than expected. This wasn't a demand problem — the activation and engagement numbers are solid — it's a conversion problem at the free-to-paid step. Mobile is the other flag: 34% of new signups come from mobile, but only 12% of MAUs are mobile users. Something is dropping mobile users after signup.

**Recommendation for Q1:** Fix the free-to-paid conversion path before adding new features. The top of the funnel is healthy; the revenue leak is in the middle. Secondary priority: investigate mobile drop-off — the gap between mobile signups and mobile retention is too large to ignore.
